Community-safe comment drafts

Indie founder discussion

One thing I am trying to avoid is treating "0 revenue" as a verdict too early. I find it more useful to ask: is the buyer moment specific enough that a stranger would bring a real question, read the free frame, and consider paying to unlock the rest? I wrote the current test case here: https://www.changeclear.com/use-cases/product-launch-zero-mrr?utm_source=community&utm_medium=comment&utm_campaign=day5_prefill_reading&utm_content=indie_founder_comment

Roadmap discussion

I would separate the request from the change it creates. If building it changes the buyer profile, support load, positioning, or roadmap gravity, it is not just a feature request anymore. This is the frame I am using: https://www.changeclear.com/use-cases/feature-request-roadmap?utm_source=community&utm_medium=comment&utm_campaign=day5_prefill_reading&utm_content=roadmap_comment

Career offer discussion

I would be careful reducing the decision to salary. The money matters, but the offer also changes pace, culture, identity, commute, stress, and future optionality. This is not advice on what to choose; it is a structure for reading the change first: https://www.changeclear.com/use-cases/high-salary-offer?utm_source=community&utm_medium=comment&utm_campaign=day5_prefill_reading&utm_content=career_offer_comment
